Infrared Astronomy and Medium-size Telescopes

3-5 March 2002, Beijing, China

*************************************************************


Dear Colleague,

An international workshop is being planned on the topic of infrared
astronomy and medium-size telescopes. Following the significant 
discussions in the East Asian Meeting of Astronomy 5 (EAMA5) in 
Taipei, this meeting is aimed at assembling optical and infrared 
astronomers in our local regions to review scientific motivation 
and to achieve common recognition for establishing medium-size 
telescopes in East Asian region. By assembling a group of 
researchers with diverse observational and theoretical expertise,
we also hope the meeting will promote realistic cooperation to 
explore the exciting theme of making the first East-Asian infrared 
telescope.

The scientific program will be framed by three sessions,
1) Infrared Astronomy: astrophysical results and highlights based on
various size telescopes, through extended and in-depth reviews and
discussions, 2) Medium-size Infrared Telescopes: examples and 
requests  in optimizing telescope design and infrared instruments, 
and 3) Astronomical Site Survey: results and plans of monitoring
astronomical sites.

The meeting will be held in Beijing at the beginning of March, 
hosted by National Astronomical Observatories (NAOC) and Purple 
Mountain Observatory (PMO), Chinese Academy of Sciences.
Registration is now open and this announcement calls for papers.
All participants are kindly requested to submit e-mail to
yqyao@pmo.ac.cn, after filling the registration form attached. 
A summary of the scientific programs and other more information 
will be sent to pre-registered participants in the second announcement.

Please feel free to disseminate this information to your colleagues.

We look forward to hearing from you.
