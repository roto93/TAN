Systems Scientist in Radio Interferometry
Australia Telescope National Facility
Locked Bag 194,
NARRABRI, NSW
AUSTRALIA

Applications are invited for a systems scientist position at the ATNF,
Australia's premier radio astronomical facility.  The position is
available at the Paul Wild Observatory, near Narrabri, 500 km north-west
of Sydney.  The successful candidate will take a significant role in the
future development and current operation of the observatory.  In
addition to these duties, 40% of time is available for personal
research.

Applicants must have a PhD degree in astronomy, astrophysics or related
discipline, and have demonstrated expertise in both the observational
and technical aspects of radio interferometry.  The commencement annual
salary is in the range of $A51K to $A62K plus benefits.  A discretionary
research allowance of $A4K per annum, support to attend overseas
scientific meetings and reasonable relocation expenses are also payable.
More information is available from

http://www.narrabri.atnf.csiro.au/jobs/sysscientist.pdf.

Applications should address the selection criteria, and should include a
curriculum vitae, list of publications, description of research
interests, and the names and addresses (preferably email) of three
referees.  Applications should be mailed to The Recruitment Officer, at
the above address, or e-mailed to kylee.forbes@csiro.au.  The closing
date for applications is 5 July 2002.  For further information on the
ATNF, see the WWW pages

http://www.atnf.csiro.au/.

For more information on this position, contact Dr R.J. Sault,
Officer-in-Charge, Paul Wild Observatory, mail: bob.sault@csiro.au,
fax: +61-2-67904090.
