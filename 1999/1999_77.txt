Subject: Olof Rydbeck (1911 - 1999)

Dear Colleagues,

It is with great sadness that we have to tell you that Olof died a week ago
on Saturday (March 27), peacefully among his family. He was 88 years old.
His funeral service will be held tomorrow at Onsala and he will be buried
in Skona
near his birthplace.

Olof was the father of Swedish radio astronomy and founder of the Onsala Space
Observatory. He was professor at Chalmers University of Technology from 1943
and held the chairs in Electronics and Electron Physics before he retired
in 1978.
He played an influencial role in the build up of the Deparrtment of
Electrical Engineering
at the University.

Olof was an inspirational leader and a fine physicist and electrical
engineer, well
known both in Sweden and  internationaly . He can even be said to have been
Sweden's first Space Researcher, having founded the Kiruna Ionospheric
Observatory
 in 1947.

After his retirement, he maintained his interest in science and took great
pleasure
in following developments in cosmology. He was, until recently, a regular
participant
in colloquia at the observatory.

He will be missed by his friends at Chalmers, the institution for Radio and
Space
Science and at Onsala Space Observatory.

Roy Booth
