Dear AIs:

I regret to tell you that WIRE is for all intents and purposes 
lost.

The longer story is this: during the opening of the secondary
vent, it seems that the cover was ejected (still a mystery how 
that could have happened).  

The premature cover ejection caused the cryogen to vaporize 
at such furious rates that the gas flowing out of the vents 
applied significant torque on the spacecraft, which spun up
faster and faster, beyond the ability of the reaction wheels
to slow it down.  

Since the spacecraft is spinning and tumbling, the solar panels 
are not getting enough energy, and the spacecraft is running on 
batteries.  We are down to about 30% of the battery load; the
cryogens will run out in a day or two.

At the moment there is some discussion of getting 
data for radiation hits.  Just to see how the arrays perform.

I am very sorry.  

I will keep you informed as more information becomes available.

Jean
