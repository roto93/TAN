>We would like to call your attention to an upcoming opportunity for
>Hubble Space Telescope (HST) observations associated with the
>possibility of a Leonid meteor storm in 1999. To minimize the risk to
>the telescope, HST pointing will be restricted to the anti-radiant
>direction of the meteroids for a period of several orbits. In order to
>make the best scientific use of this opportunity, STScI will prepare a
>service observing program. Some restrictions on the observations are
>as follows:
>
>  1) The interval for the observations is November 17th, 1999 22:30 UT -
>	November 18th, 1999 06:30 UT.
>  2) The pointing will be restricted to within 1 degree of the direction 
>	of the average anti-radiant of the meteroids as observed from HST.
>	That direction is 336.2 degrees RA and -23.3 degrees DEC. 
>  3) The observations may not place any restrictions on the HST roll
>        angle (such as ORIENT restrictions).
>  4) The program will be about 5 orbits.
>  5) No specific amount of data will be guaranteed.
>  6) The observations will have no proprietary time nor any funding
>	associated for data analysis.
>  7) There is no guarantee that this service program will indeed execute.
>
>We invite your advice or suggestions for the science objective(s)
>and/or possible targets. Please contact Dr. Duccio Macchetto,
>Associate Director for Science Programs, (macchetto@stsci.edu) with
>your input prior to October 8, 1999.
