{\bf Abstract}

%=================================================================
%  please type the abstract text here  (maximum 200 words)
%=================================================================
 When
 observed with high angular resolution jets
 in quasars and AGN show structure with
 bright knots separated by dark regions.
 Radiation of knots is usually strongly
 polarized, indicating to the synchrotron radiation
 of the relativistic
 electrons in an ordered magnetic fields. Estimation of the life
 time of these electrons, based on the observed luminosities and
 spectra, gives values much less than their kinematic ages $d/c$,
 where $d$ is the distance of the emitting point from the central
 source.
 That indicates to a necessity of acceleration
 of electrons in the jets in order to explain the observations.
 If a charge separation happens at the formation of knots, long wave
 electromagnetic oscillations proceed, leading to emission of
 a strong electromagnetic waves.
 So, charged particles are directly
 accelerated by the electric field of the waves.
