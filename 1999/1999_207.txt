The Max-Planck Institut fuer extraterrestrische Physik (MPE) in Garching
near Munich invites applications for
 
      a tenure track, staff research scientist position
 
in observational infrared astronomy with a starting date in summer 2000. 
Candidates should have a strong research background in observational
infrared/optical, extragalactic astronomy.  Applicants with additional
experience in infrared instrumentation and/or general instrumentation
project development are particularly welcome. 
 
The appointed scientist is expected to take a leading role in the
development and execution of the major astronomical and experimental
programs underway or in the planning stages in the infrared/submillimeter
group of MPE. The group's main research centers around infrared and
millimeter spectroscopic and high resolution imaging programs of the
Galactic Center, AGN and starburst nuclei, galaxy mergers, and high-z
galaxies.  These research activities are supported largely by a parallel
program of innovative infrared instrumentation development.  Current in
house instruments include a 1-2.5 micron field imaging spectrometer, a
1-2.5 micron speckle camera, and an adaptive optics system with laser
beacons at the 3.5 m telescope on Calar Alto, Spain. Starting in 2000-2001
the group will commence science operations of a high-resolution IR camera
(CONICA) and a new NIR field imaging spectrometer (SINFONI) on the
ESO-VLT.  Future projects include major instruments for FIRST and SOFIA
(2002). Starting in 2003 MPE will have guaranteed access to the 2x8.4m
Large Binocular Telescope (LBT), in addition to the present regular access
to the facilities at IRAM and ESO (NTT and VLT). 

The position is for an initial period of three years, with a likely
extension to five years. Tenure is possible thereafter but in exceptional
cases could be considered earlier. The starting annual salary is based on
the standard German BAT scale for civil servants and depends on experience, 
age and family situation. Applicants should have a Ph.D. in astronomy, 
physics or a related field and several years postdoctoral experience. For 
further information about the group see http://www.mpe-garching.mpg.de/
infrarot.html. 
 
To apply, please send a letter of application, a brief description of
research interests, a curriculum vitae including bibliography and at least
three letters of reference by 15 December 1999 to Dr. Reinhard Genzel at
the following address:

Prof. Reinhard Genzel
Max-Planck-Institut fuer extraterrestrische Physik
Postfach 1603
85740 Garching
Germany
 
The MPE especially encourages applications from women and minorities.
