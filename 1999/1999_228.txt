The Symposium on Space Terahertz Technology features the latest research from
the U.S.A. and abroad pertaining to the detection, generation, and use of
terahertz radiation for astronomy, solar system studies, remost sensing of
Earth's atmosphere, and other applications.  Topics of interest for the 11th
International Symposium include:

	Schottky diode mixer and multiplier technology
	SIS mixer technology
	Hot Electron Bolometers
	Antennas, coupling structures and other passive circuits
	Overviews of space based and astrophysics THz applications
	Direct detection techniques
	Pulsed THz technology
	Non-space applications such as remote senging
	Other topics of interest to the THz community

Authors wishing to submit a papers should submit a one to two-page
abstract.  The abstract should emphasize the new and unique features
of the research.  The dealine for receipt of abstracts is January 31,
1999.  Notification of acceptance will be sent by email by February

papers.  Final papers will be due May 1, 1999.  Abstracts should
