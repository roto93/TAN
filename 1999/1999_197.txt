******************************* TECHNICAL NEWS *******************************

!!!!!!!!!!SAO scientists confirm Young's 1803 double-slit interference
experiment with novel instrumental setup. Wave nature of light validated!!!!!!!
            
Hawaii: As most of the world knows, beautiful, strong, sinuous and steady 
fringes were obtained on a variety of sources with various correlators 
starting the night of SEP 29. After several days of struggle the cryostat 
in Antenna 3 was finally persuaded to cool down properly, and the spare
230 GHz mixer that had been installed on SEP 26 worked fine. The first 
target was the 230 GHz beacon on the Subaru catwalk, which produced good 
results. The phase changed smoothly as the secondary reflection on 
Antenna 3 was moved. The next task was to calibrate the pointing on 
Antenna 3. The first look at Jupiter produced pointing offsets of 10 
and 5 arcseconds in azimuth and elevation; very small offsets from the 
pointing model derived with the holography receiver. The first fringes on 
a celestial source were obtained on Saturn with the simple multiplier at 
11:14 pm HST (about 25 hours ahead of the deadline promised to Congress). 
The fringes were recorded for posterity on our trusty analog chart recorder 
on loan from Bell Labs, running at 15 cm/minute. Saturn had an angular size 
of about 20 arcseconds, and the fringe spacing was 10 arcseconds at 230 GHz 
for our 25-meter E-W baseline. Our detection came when the visibility on 
Saturn was rather close to a null, but the signal strength was so great that 
this caused little problem (see detailed visibility models of the planets 
on SEP 29 on http://cfa-www.harvard.edu/~mgurwell/planetary_flux.html).
A few hours later whopping fringes were recorded on Venus, which was 
also heavily resolved (Venus's diameter was 37 arcseconds). The image of 
these fringes are on the SMA website. Note the sausage pattern of 
amplitude modulation caused by our double sideband system (a null every 
23 fringes for a sideband separation of 10 GHz and a center frequency of 
230 GHz). The next night the Timoc correlator was connected and fringes were 
detected on the CO (2-1) line in Orion. Less than an hour later the 
fringe spectrum was measured with the MIT correlator configured to analyze
one chunk of bandwidth (104 MHz) with 8 chips (about 0.5 MHz resolution). 
The spectrum on the website is similar to the one obtained at Haystack last 
fall; it shows a double peak since the ambient CO is completely resolved, 
leaving only the bipolar outflow components. Fringes were also observed
on Jupiter and Mars.

Most of the visiting Cambridge crew members were totally exhausted and headed
for the airport on Friday, leaving the exhausted resident staff and a few
others to continue taking data.     

Haystack: Late Friday afternoon the velocity loop was closed on the 
new elevation axis servo on Antenna 1. The motion was very smooth! The
limit encoder is connected to the system control board and the system
shuts down if the antenna moves outside the limits (currently 20-85 degrees). 
The Glentek enable function and brake release are both working and the 
dynamic brake is connected. This week the position loop will be closed.

The first 10 panels for the prototype cabin arrived at Haystack.
