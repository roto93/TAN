2. URSI/USNC Meeting - Jan 5-8, 2000 - Announcement from Julie McKie

The Web Page for the 2000 URSI meeting in Boulder, Colorado, is now available 
on the web. Unlike in preceding years, the Radar Techniques Workshop will be 
held BEFORE the meeting on Tuesday, January 4.  The regular meeting will begin 
on Wednesday, January 5 and continue through Saturday, January 8.  The 
Call-for-Papers has been mailed but if you are not on the mailing list you can 
still get the Call by downloading the PDF file from the web page. (You will 
need Adobe Acrobat Reader to download it). The Call this year is considerably
briefer than in past years since the web page has become our primary tool for 
communication.  The web page at http://cires.colorado.edu/ursi contains much 
greater information than the Call-for-Papers.

This e-mail went to those people who submitted papers as the author of contact 
for URSI 1999. If you know of colleagues who may not have received this e-mail, 
or who may not have web access, please print off a copy of the Call-for-Papers 
for them. I don't know at this time if we will have on-line registration.  We 
are working on it, but at this time it is not up and running.  We'll communicate 
about this in later e-mails, on the website, and in the technical program, which 
goes out in November.

Please remember: The deadline for receipt of abstract is SEPTEMBER 3, 1999.  
No papers will be accepted after that date. 

As always, if you have any questions, please write to us at ursi@cires.colorado.edu.

4. International workshop on radio methods for studying turbulence
University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign,     August 9-12, 1999

Turbulence in geophysical and astrophysical, neutral and ionized, 
media will be the subject of this workshop. It is intended to 
meet the general need for an exchange of information and ideas 
between scientists working on turbulence in the atmosphere, 
ionosphere, solar wind and interstellar medium, with emphasis on 
radio probing methods. 

There will be Invited presentations on ISM and IPM turbulence.
Contributed talks are encouraged on these and related topics.

Deadline for Abstracts is JUNE 1, 1999 - in the USNC/URSI format 
(example on the web site), limited to 2 pages and 
emailed to Prof. A. W. Wernik:  aww@cbk.waw.pl   
with a copy to Joyce Mast: jmast@uiwpls.ece.uiuc.edu 

For complete up-to-date Workshop information visit
www.ece.uiuc.edu/news/ursi/ursi1.html on the WEB.
