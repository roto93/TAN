Date: 14:00, 22/Sep/2006
Loc.: Room 1013, Science Building IV, National Central University

Speaker: Dr. Nishioka Hiroaki (ASIAA)

Title: Quadrupole power spectra for the 2dF QSO and future redshift surveys

Abstract:
Redshift-space distortions due to peculiar velocities and space-time
geometry causes anisotropy of the galaxy distribution with respect to
line-of-sight direction and perpendicular to it.  The quadrupole power
spectrum is one of the useful statistics to quantify the anisotorpy
and detect the signature of the redshift-space distortions.
We develop an estimator for the quadrupole power spectrum starting
from a discrete data set for a redshift survey and show algorism to
compute the quadrupole power spectrum. We apply our estimator to 2dF
QSO redshift survey and measure the quarupole power spectrum.  The
best-fit bias parameter (the relation between background matter and
QSO distributions) from the measured power spectrum is consistent with
the previous results obtained by other approach, although measured
quadrupole power spectrum is noisy due to poor data set.
We also show the quadrupole power spectrum will be useful to constrain
the property of dark energy for the future redsfhit surveys using the
Fisher matrix approach assuming the WFMOS (former KAOS) sample.
