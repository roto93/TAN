>             Winter Workshop on Strongly coupled Coulomb systems
> =========================================================================
>
> Date : January 28-30, 2002
> Place: Lecture room 620, Physics Building, National Tsing Hua University
>
> Under extreme circumstances Coulomb interactions between charged particles
> become as strong as kinetic energy and the system becomes "strongly
> interacting". It leads to a qualitaively new static and dynamic phenomena
> like crystallization.  Examples are electron gas (2D or 3D with or without
> magnetic field) at "Wigner crystallization" density or filling factor,
> "dusty" plasma - collection of highly charged particles in plasma, plasma
> in certain astrophysical systems and colloidal suspensions. The systems
> present not only experimenta - also theoretical challange, since typically
> one has to develop nonperturbative methods.
>
