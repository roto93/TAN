Scientific Motivation
---------------------
 Our knowledge of galaxy dynamics has made big steps in recent years.
From a theoretical point of view, numerical simulations, with always
increasing computing power, are more and more precise, and now include
several essential phenomena, such as gas hydrodynamics, or star formation
and feedback.
From an observational point of view, the high spatial resolution provided by HST
and the new generation of large ground-based telescopes, beginning with the
Keck, have made possible new discoveries on internal galaxy dynamics 
(galaxy nuclei), and high-redshift evolution. 

The aim of this conference is to develop these two aspects, to summarize 
recent advances in galaxy dynamics, and to emphasize the unsolved problems 
and new directions to investigate with future large instruments (such as VLT, 
XMM, LSA, FIRST, NGST), as well as with the new generation of supercomputers.


The main topics addressed will be:
---------------------------------

Dynamics of bars and circumnuclear disks 

Dark matter and gas accretion

Formation and evolution of disks

Formation of bulges and ellipticals

Galaxy interactions and merging

Dynamics of galaxies in clusters

Hubble sequence evolution

Theoretical and instrumental perspectives
