Subject: #3 NSC travel fund -- my 2 cents

As some of TANists may know already, the current NSC policy for funding
oversea observing trips is quite unreasonable.  I am glad that the
issue was finally brought to the open.  Thanks to Darwin Chang
(even though he is leaving NSC and we astronomers can only get 5 cents
from this particular physicist:-)  IMHO, I think Darwin's plan is very
good and is in practice feasible as long as Director Ku doesn't object.
The question for us NOW is "should we do it or not?"

Here are a few things I think which might need to be strsightened out first:

a) The AS contribution.  We need to work out some kind of formula here
obviously and that will not be easy.  For instance, If AS does decide 
to join the plan and contribute some money to the fund, then will
NSC grant the travel money to (AS) postdoc fellows?

b) The initial funding from NSC.  What is the current level of NSC travel
funding?  How many oversea observing trips is it equivalent to?  If the 
number falls on the low side, say, a single digit, then it won't be very
helpful at all and apparently it won't attract AS to join either.

c) The process time.  How fast can we get the application approved?
Many international observatories review the observing proposals 
by seasons.  In other words, the turn-around time between proposal
approvals and the actual observing date sometime can be quite short
(less than one month in some extreme cases).  So a quick response
time would be a critical factor for the plan.

That's my 2 cents.

Yi-Jehng
