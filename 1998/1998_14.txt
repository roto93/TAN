                Colloquium of Paris-Meudon Observatory

                          Galaxy evolution:=20
     Connecting the distant Universe with the Local Fossil Record

Meudon,  France, September 21-25, 1998=20
**********************************************************************


DATE AND VENUE

The colloquium will be held at the Paris Observatory at Meudon,
France, on September 21-25, 1998.
It will be held in honour of Roger an Giusa Cayrel, with=20
the topic being chosen in order to recognize the contributions=20
that they have made to the study of the galactic evolution and in=20
particular the early phases of the evolution of our Galaxy.
The colloqium is sponsored by Observatoire de Paris, CFH telescope,
and INSU-CNRS.

SCOPE OF THE COLLOQUIUM

The purpose is to bring together astronomers working in the=20
field of the galaxies evolution (Milky Way and external=20
galaxies), to review recent developments and constrain the=20
models of the evolution of the galaxies using the properties of=20
the old galactic components:
   -the spherical halo
   -the thick disk
   -the Bulge
We would like to encourage the participants to address in=20
particular the work which is now in the process of being done,=20
with the arrival of the new generation of the 8m-10m=20
telescopes.
Several groups all over the world are actively involved in this=20
type of research, and by combining different expertises it=20
would be possible to achieve an optimal use of the very large=20
telescopes.
Exchange of information between different research groups, as=20
well as the promotion of a coordinated approach are essential=20
for this purpose.


OUTLINE OF THE SCIENTIFIC PROGRAM

      -Pregalactic evolution and nucleosynthesis
      -Key features of stellar evolution
      -Formation and evolution of galactic  halos=20
          (Milky Way, External galaxies)
      -The old disk component=20
          (Milky Way, External galaxies)
      -Formation and evolution of galactic Bulges
          (Milky Way, External galaxies)
      -Future Prospects
