Subject: Job Vacancy

The National Radio Astronomy Observatory (NRAO) has the following job
vacancy at its site in Green Bank, West Virginia.  This site provides a
stimulating intellectual environment in a picturesque rural location in
Pocahontas County, not far from the Snowshoe Ski Resort. 

The AIPS++ Project is an international collaboration by a consortium of
radio observatories, managed by the NRAO with the goal of developing a
software package for the processing of radio astronomical data. 
Investigation and development of algorithms to run on parallel computers
is being pursued within the AIPS++ Project by the NRAO and the National
Center for Supercomputing Applications (NCSA), with funding from the
NCSA. 

CLASSIFICATION:  	ASSISTANT SCIENTIST 
REQUISITION NO.:                SO2233

DESCRIPTION:  
Participates in the design and coding of Green Bank Telescope (GBT) data
reduction software, which is being written in the AIPS++ system. Assists
and advises astronomers on the use of AIPS++ software, and manages the
local AIPS++ installation.  Pursuit of astronomical research is expected
(25% research time). 

QUALIFICATIONS:  	
Applicants must have a Ph.D. in Astronomy or related field, with an
interest and experience in software development.  Experience in the use
of single dish radio telescopes is required.  Some familiarity with
object-oriented methods and C++ is desirable.  Experience in a large
software project would be advantageous.

Further information is available at the NRAO website address:
http://www.nrao.edu.  Application materials can be sent to: Personnel
Office, NRAO, PO Box 0, Socorro, NM 87801 or email to:
tmcbride@nrao.edu.
