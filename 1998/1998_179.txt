  About the Taiwan-USA NGI/vBNS Project
     The current bandwidth between Taiwan and USA, 3Mbps by DS1(T1)x2,
     is already over-consumed;
     Expectedly in Nov. 1998,
     TANet will upgrade its USA link to DS3(T3) bandwidth, 45 Mbps,
     with two-thirds of the bandwidth intended "solely" for higher
     education and research use. 

  What is TANet?
     Taiwan Academic Network, inaugurated in 1990, connects all major
     education and research institutions in Taiwan.

  What is Mbps, DS1, and DS3?
     Mbps: Mega (1,000,000) bits per second. (Note: "bit" not "byte")
     DS1 (DS3): A dedicated phone connection supports data rate 
                1.5 (45) Mbps; a T1 (T3) line actually consists of 
                24  (672) individual channels, each of which supports 
                64 Kbps.
