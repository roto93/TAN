Opening for position of Postdoctoral Research Fellow (PDRF) for the 
James Clerk Maxwell Telescope (JCMT) at the Joint Astronomy Centre (JAC) 
in Hilo, Hawaii initially for a period of one year.  The PDRF will be 
expected to work closely with JAC staff on a research programme 
involving observations made with the JCMT.  Duties:  undertake an 
original research programme, primarily using the Submillimetre Common 
User Bolometer Array (SCUBA) camera; participate in the on-going 
development of SCUBA on the telescope, and undertake service observing 
programmes or support visiting astronomers as required.   Requirements:  
Ph.D. in Physics, Astronomy or a closely related subject.  
(Consideration will also be given to students who are close to 
completing a thesis and can demonstrate ability, interest, and skill).  
Good background in scientific computing.  Previous experience in 
astronomy related research.  Sound knowledge of astronomical observing 
techniques and ability to carry out independent research, without close 
supervision.  Good written and verbal communication skills and ability 
to interact well with others.  Must hold a valid driving license for 4-
wheel drive vehicles with manual transmission.  Must pass a high 
altitude medical examination.

Further information regarding duties, responsibilities and 
qualifications from Dr. Wayne S. Holland (JAC), (808) 969-6565; e-mail 
w.holland@jach.hawaii.edu.

Application Requirements:  cover letter (note Rec. # ID98281), Resume, 
Salary History, three work related references, and copy of degree(s) to 
the Director Human Resources at the above address.  Closing Date:

30 November 1998.  Recruitment No. 98281.
