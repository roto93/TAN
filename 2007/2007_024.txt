>> This is the first announcement of the 3rd Japan-Taiwan ALMA Science

>> meeting will be the National Central University of Taiwan.

>> http://chunjiao.astro.ncu.edu.tw/almajt3/.

>> WORKSHOP FORMAT
>>
>> Now astronomers in Japan and Taiwan are putting their efforts together
>> to the ALMA project. In order to accelerate the discussion on the
>> science and collaboration between Japan and Taiwan, Japan-Taiwan ALMA
>> Science Meetings are held every 9 months since the end of 2005.
>>
>> The 3rd Japan-Taiwan ALMA Science Meeting will be 2-day workshop on
>> 13-14 April 2007. The meeting is jointly hosted by the Institute of
>> Astronomy at National Central University and the Institute of
>> Astronomy and Astrophysics at Academia Sinica in Taiwan. The program
>> includes status reports of the project, recent activities in Japan and
>> Taiwan, and progress reports from sub-working groups as well as
>> scientific talks. New scientific topics and fresh idea on the
>> collaboration that were not discussed at previous meetings are highly
>> welcome. This time a special session focused on the planetary science
>> is also planned. This meeting will be the first Japan-Taiwan ALMA
>> Science Meeting hosted by the university. We encourage graduate
>> students to join the meeting and give oral or poster
>> presentations. There is no registration fee.
>>
>> The arrangement of sub-working group meetings before the main workshop
>> is strongly recommended. (See below for the detail.)
>>
>> VENUE
>>
>> The venue of the meeting will be the National Central University. The
>> University is located in Chungli. It is about 30 minutes drive from
>> Taoyuan International Airport, and is about 10 minutes drive from the
>> downtown of Chungli city. The workshop will be held at the multi-media
>> room of the Grand Lecture Hall. It is located near the main gate of
>> the university, and it has a capacity of 100 person.
>>
>>   National Central University: http://www.ncu.edu.tw/
>>   Campus map: http://www.ncu.edu.tw/images/ncumap.pdf
