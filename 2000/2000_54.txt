Dear Colleague,

The time is ripe for a meeting on the ISM in M31 and M33. Quite a
number
of new data sets on these nearby spirals recently have or soon will
become available - Radio continuum, X-rays, H-alpha, Infrared, CO,
optical
extinction- and it therefore seems appropriate to bring the various
researchers together to see and discuss all these data.

The meeting will take place in the Physikzentrum in Bad Honnef near
Bonn,
Germany, May 21 to 25 next year. As the available accomodation
restricts
the number of participants to about 60, the meeting will have the
character of a workshop for researchers who are working or recently
have been
working on the ISM of M31, M33 or who are interested in this topic.
The aim is
to compare, combine and discuss the results from the various
wavelength
ranges in order to improve our understanding of what is going on in
the ISM.
Comparisons between properties of the ISM in M31, M33, the Milky Way
and
other nearby galaxies will also be useful.

The meeting will cover the following main fields:
I   The neutral gas
II  Dust and Extinction
III The warm ionized gas
IV  The hot ionized gas
V   Cosmic rays and magnetic fields

Each field or subfield will be introduced by an invited speaker (see
list below) and be followed by short contributions. Also posters will
be
displayed.

The meeting is sponsored by the WE-Heraeus Stiftung which will cover
the
basic costs of your stay. The WE-Heraeus Stiftung will send the
official
invitations. This circular is meant to probe which researchers would
be
interested to attend.

If you are interested please fill out the attached form before 10
JANUARY 2000
and return it to

                             eberkhuijsen@mpifr-bonn.mpg.de


With best regards,

Elly M. Berkhuijsen (MPIfR, Bonn, Germany)
Rainer Beck (MPIfR, Bonn, Germany)
