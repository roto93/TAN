                    Research Fellow/Postdoctoral Positions
                      NATIONAL ASTRONOMICAL OBSERVATORY
                                JAPAN (NAOJ)

Applications are invited for research fellow and/or postdoctoral positions at
NAOJ under the Center of Excellence Foreign Research Fellows Program of the
Ministry of Education, Science, Sports and Culture. The term of appointment
will be from 3 to 12 months in 2001 (April 2001 -- March 2002) with possibility
of extension. The monthly salary (including tax) will be between 368,000 and
905,000 Japanese Yen (approx. US$ 3,500 - 8,600)) depending on qualifications
and place of work. Expenses will be covered  for a round trip economy class
travel to Japan as well as for a limited number of domestic travels for
scientific conferences and discussions.

Possible research areas include:

1. Radio Astronomy
Engineering and software developments of the 45-m telescope, the millimeter
array, the space VLBI mission (VSOP), the radioheliograph , receivers, and
the planned Large Millimeter and Submillimeter Array (LMSA) under negotiation
to make a large world array with the Atacama Large Millimeter Array (ALMA) in
northern Chile, and Observational study on interstellar matter, star formation,
galaxies, AGN, cosmology, and solar physics using the facilities. The 
successful
candidate will spend 50% of his/her time on the developments and operation
of the facilities and the rest for his/her independent personal research (http:
//www.nro.nao.ac.jp/index-e.html).

Contact person : Professor NAKAI Naomasa, Nobeyama Radio Observatory,
Minamimaki, Minamisaku, Nagano 384-1305, JAPAN (e-mail: nakai@nro.nao.ac.jp)


2. VLBI and Planetary Science
Scientific perspectives and technology/software developments for
1)  Project VERA(VLBI Exploration of Radio Astrometry) which aims at 10 micro-
arcsecond level phase-referenced VLBI astrometry for making a three-dimensional
map of the Milky Way Galaxy and is now under construction of initial three
stations to be completed in 2001 (http://veraserver.mtk.nao.ac.jp), or
2)  Project RISE (Research in SElenodesy) aiming at uncovering internal 
structure
and history of the Moon in terms of high precision geodetic measurements in
Japanese Lunar Mission SELENE (SELEnological and Engineering Explorer) to be
launched in 2004 (http://www.miz.nao.ac.jp/future-e.html#Fplan).

Contact person : Professor SASAO Tetsuo, National Astronomical Observatory,
2-21-1 Osawa, Mitaka, Tokyo, 181-8588 JAPAN (e-mail: sasao@miz.nao.ac.jp)
