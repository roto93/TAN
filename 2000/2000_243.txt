3-5 year Post-Doctoral Position at the MPIA in Heidelberg
==========================================================================
 
The Max Planck Institute for Astronomy (MPIA) in Heidelberg is offering
a
post-doctoral position to a highly-motivated, outstanding researcher,
who
is given the opportunity to independently carry out observational
programs in extragalactic astronomy and cosmology. The MPIA is a partner
in the ongoing Sloan Digital Sky Survey (SDSS) and the successful
candidate is encouraged to pursue projects that draw on SDSS data.
The MPIA operates Calar Alto Observatory, with its 2.2m and 3.5m
telescopes, has access to ESO's VLT and La Silla Telescopes, and is a
partner in the 2x8.4m LBT Project on Mount Graham. The MPIA is leading
the development of a number of ground and space-based instruments.
Further information on the science interests of the MPIA community
can be found at http://www.mpia-hd.mpg.de/Public/index_en.html.
The initial appointment will be made for three years, with yearly
extensions possible to a maximum of five years. Funds for travel,
publication and observing will be available. Interested candidates
should send application materials, including curriculum vita and a
brief statement of research interests to the address below. They
should also arrange for three letters of recommendation to be sent
by 15 December 2000.

Send application materials by 15 December 2000 to
 
Dr. Hans-Walter Rix, 
Max-Planck-Institute fuer Astronomie, 
Koenigstuhl 17, 
69117 Heidelberg, Germany.

The Max Planck Society is an equal opportunity employer and
particularly
encourages applications from women.
