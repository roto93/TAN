                  From: APJ, Vol.528, 2000 January 10
                  Auther: Bradford B. Behr, ... 

                  We have determined the projected rotational velocities of 13 blue horizontal-branch (BHB) stars in the globular cluster M13 via rotational broadening of metal absorption lines. Our sample spans the photometric gap observed in the horizontal-branch distribution at Teff 11,000 K and reveals a pronounced difference in stellar rotation on either side of this featureblueward of the gap, all the stars show modest rotations, v sin i < 10 km s-1, while to the red side of the gap, we confirm the more rapidly rotating population (vsini 40 km/s) previously observed by R. C. Peterson and coworkers. Taken together with these prior results, our measurements indicate that a star's rotation is indeed related to its location along the HB, although the mechanism behind this correlation remains nknown. We explore possible connections between stellar rotation and mass-loss mechanisms which influence the photometric morphology of globular cluster HBs.
