 Postdoctoral Positions to work on the Sloan Digital Sky Survey at MPA
 =====================================================================




The Max-Planck-Institut fuer Astrophysik (MPA) is a major German 
institute dedicated primarily to theoretical research in astrophysics. 
It is situated a few kilometers north of the centre of Munich on a 
campus shared with the MPI fuer Extraterrestrische Physik, the head-
quarters of the European Southern Observatory, the MPI fuer Plasmaphysik, 
the Technical University of Munich, and several other research institutes. 
The MPA has about fifteen permanent staff members who work with a somewhat 
larger number of postdoctoral researchers and with about 25 graduate 
students; it also runs a vigorous visitor programme. The institute is 
housed in an attractive, specially designed building which contains 
excellent library and computer facilities. It also has privileged access 
to supercomputer facilities at the neighboring MPP Computer Centre. 
Further information about the institute can be found on our web page:
          http://www.mpa-garching.mpg.de 

In November 2000 the MPA completed negociations to join the Sloan Digital 
Sky Survey project. As a result a group of MPA scientists will have the 
opportunity to participate fully in this extremely promising survey which 
has now been successfully gathering survey data for a number of months 
(see http://www.sdss.org). In its current postdoctoral competition (see 
advertisement #11566 in the AAS Job Register) the MPA will be particularly 
interested in recruiting candidates with scientific interests and expertise 
relevant for working on SDSS-related projects. As with other MPA postdocs
successful candidates will normally be appointed for three years which
can be extended up to a total of five.  Annual salary is in the range 
65,000 - 80,000 DM before tax, depending on age and family status. 

Interested scientists are invited to send a full curriculum vitae, a 
publication list and a summary of current research interests to 

The Appointments Committee,
MAX-PLANCK-INSTITUT FUER ASTROPHYSIK
Karl-Schwarzschild-Str. 1
85748 Garching/Munich, Germany.

They should also arrange for three letters of reference to be sent to
this address. The deadline for application is

December 15 2000

but the institute may consider any complete application received before all 
posts are filled.
