The Symposium on Space Terahertz Technology features the latest research=
 from the U.S.A. and abroad pertaining to the detection, generation, and use=
 of terahertz radiation for astronomy, solar system studies, remote sensing=
 of Earth's atmosphere, and other applications.  Topics of interest for the=
 12th International Symposium include but are not limited to:

Heterodyne detection techniques
	Mixers, including Schottky diode, SIS, and hot electron bolometers
	Sources, including multipliers, photomixers, lasers
	Direct detection techniques
	Detector technologies, including multiplexed readouts
	Novel devices, both semiconductor and superconductor
 	Antennas, coupling structures, and other passive components/circuits
	Optical components and methods
	Properties of materials
 	Pulsed THz technology
	Instrument systems and architectures
 	THz Space projects and applications, astrophysics and remote sensing
 	Non-space projects and applications
Micromachining/MEMS Techniques for THz
 	Other topics of interest to the THz community

 mailed soon.  The 12th International Symposium on Space Terahertz=
 Technology will immediately follow the FIRST Science Workshop (more details=
 follow) that will be held at the same location on February 12 and 13, 2001.=
  This arrangement was made to facilitate interaction between the Technology=
 and Science communities.

1st Announcement
Submillimeter Astronomy Science Workshop
for Far Infrared Submillimeter Telescope (FIRST)

FIRST Science Workshop - 12th and 13th February, 2001

A Workshop will be held at Humphrey's Half Moon Inn on Shelter Island, San=
=20
Diego, 12th and 13th February, 2001, to help inform the US astronomy=20
community of the scientific opportunities available with the FIRST mission.

TeraHertz Technology Symposium - 14th, 15th  and 16th of February, 2001

The science workshop will be followed (14th, 15th  and 16th of February,=20
2001) by the annual TeraHertz Technology Symposium, where the relevant=20
technology will be discussed.
=B7       Participants will be welcome to attend either or both components.
=B7       A visit to Composite Optics Inc. will be arranged between the two=
=20
sections.

FIRST Mission

"FIRST" is an ESA "Cornerstone Mission" in the Horizon 2000 program,=20
consisting of a passively cooled 3.5m dia. telescope, operating in the=20
60-600 micron range at the L2 point. There are three instruments in the=20
helium cooled focal plane:
=B7       SPIRE, a long wavelength camera and low resolution spectrometer;
=B7       PACS, a short wavelength camera and low resolution spectrometer;=
 and
=B7       HIFI, a high resolution (heterodyne) spectrometer.

NASA Role

NASA is a partner in the program and has developed appropriate
technologies for the Telescope, plus focal plane detector components
for HIFI and SPIRE. JPL is the NASA lead center. NASA will support US=20
science participation through a FIRST Science Data Center at IPAC.

Format of Workshop

The format of the science workshop will be Introductory talks, followed by=
=20
structured discussions. The preliminary list of topics includes distant=20
galaxies, surveys, local galaxies, the ISM, star formation, line-surveys,=20
and the relation of FIRST to ALMA and SOFIA.

Contacts

The organizing committee is T. Phillips(chair), P. Goldsmith, P. Harvey,
M. Harwit, G. Helou, W. Langer. Queries and expressions of interest
should be sent to charmaine.d.mayes@JPL.NASA.gov
