Dear Colleague,

This is a reminder that with the event of ten years since the
launch of the Hubble Space Telescope (occurring on April 24,
2000), the Space Telescope Science Institute will devote its
annual (normally in May) spring Symposium to:  "A Decade of HST
Science."  This Symposium will review many of the main
scientific results obtained with HST, their impact on astronomy
and astrophysics, and their meaning for future research.  There
will also be a special presentation by Servicing Mission
Astronauts Claude Nicollier and John Grunsfeld.

The format for this symposium will consist of 30 minute talks,
including ample time for discussion following each talk.  There
will also be poster papers.  The topics for the talks and all of

the speakers have been chosen.  A provisional agenda of the
program is enclosed.

We do feel strongly, that the Symposium will greatly benefit if
you will participate.

If you can and would like to attend the symposium we encourage
you to register electronically as soon as possible (at
http://www.stsci.edu/isd/Decade/decade.htm).
If you have questions, you may contact:
Lorraine Garcia, Symposium Coordinator
(email:  garcia@stsci.edu)
Space Telescope Science Institute
3700 San Martin Drive
Baltimore, MD  21218.
Phone:  (410) 338 4402

To help defray some of the workshop expenses there is a
registration fee of $200 ($225 after March 1, 2000,) which
includes the symposium dinner but does not include the
proceedings.  If you plan to present a poster paper please
attach the title.

We hope very much to see you here in Baltimore in April.

Sincerely,
The Organizing Committee
