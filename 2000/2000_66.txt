            Colloquium (2:00 pm)

            SPEAKER: Prof. Howard Yee 
                     (University of Toronto)
	    TITLE:   "A Wide-Field Galaxy Cluster Survey: 
	              Cosmology and Cluster Evolution"
	              
	    ABSTRACT: 
	    This is a talk on my latest large survey, using the CFHT12K camera
and the CTIO4m 8K camera.  The imaging survey covers 100 sq deg using
an innovative 2-color technique to find rich clusters at z between 0.3
to 1.5. The goals are: (1) to derive the evolution of cluster mass spectrum
which allows us to measure the cosmological parameters Omega_m and Sigma_8;
(2) to study cluster formation and evolution, and (3) to provide a complete
sample of several hundred high-redshift clusters for other cluster
investigations. The data are now about 40% complete, and I will present
some preliminary results. This survey may be of interest to ASIAA, since
ASIAA is planning to do a S-Z effect cluster survey with AMIBA.
