A post-doctoral fellowship is available for three years starting from
August 2000 or as soon as possible thereafter to work on analysis and
follow-up of the INT Wide Area Survey. This is a survey of
more than  100 square degrees of sky in several optical passbands
to a depth of approximately r=25 being carried out by a consortium of
UK universities. The survey is an ideal basis for many studies in
observational cosmology and extragalactic astronomy, it is expected
that the fellow will help define the exact areas of study based upon
their own interests and the parameters of the survey, and will take a
lead in follow-up observations of the survey.

Applicants should have a PhD in a relevant field of astrophysics,
preferably with experience in image processing and data analysis.  The
post, within the recently expanded Bristol Astrophysics Group in the
Department of Physics, will be for up to 3 years, is on the RA1A scale
and is funded by a grant from the Leverhulme Trust.  Informal
enquiries may be directed to Dr. Malcolm Bremer, Department of Physics
(Tel: 44 (0)117-928-8764; e-mail: m.bremer@bristol.ac.uk).

For further details contact the Personnel Office, to whom applications
(quoting reference 6272) consisting of a CV and description of
research interests should be sent by  31 May 2000.  Applicants
should arrange for two referees to send letters of reference by the
deadline.

Further Particulars


The Physics Department is housed in the H.H. Wills Physics Laboratory,
and currently has 45 HEFCE-funded academic staff.  It was graded 5A 
(the highest possible) in the last research selectivity exercise.  
There are presently 40 technicians and 7 secretaries as well as 
post-doctoral workers and academic visitors.  It teaches some 350 
undergraduates and 70 postgraduates in the various Physics programmes.

The Astrophysics group, with which the successful applicant will be
working, is one of 8 research groups in the Department whose
activities also include theoretical physics, the physics of magnetic
materials at low temperatures, liquid and amorphous solids, polymer
physics, microstructural analysis, and particle physics.

There are a total of 19 staff and research students in the
Astrophysics group, including Professor Birkinshaw, Drs. Boyce,
Bremer, Hardcastle, Liang, Masheder, Phillipps, Walker, and Worrall in
Physics Bristol's well-established research programmes in Galactic
astrophysics concentrate on observations and interpretation of the
large-scale distribution of CO in the Galaxy's interstellar medium,
and theoretical and observational studies of the physics and chemistry
of masers.  The extragalactic work involves surveys for high-redshift
galaxies and clusters, low surface-brightness galaxies, work on the
Sunyaev-Zel'dovich (SZ) effect in clusters of galaxies, and X-ray and
complementary multifrequency studies of galaxies and AGN.  The
research effort is supported by the Starlink Node at Bristol, based on
a dual processor Sun Enterprise 250 computer feeding other Sun
computers, PCs and X-terminals.

The successful candidate will work on analysis and follow-up of the
INT Wide Area Survey (for a description see
http://www.ast.cam.ac.uk/~wfcsur/). The survey is suitable for many
studies in observational cosmology and extragalactic astronomy. It is
expected the successful candidate will have a strong influence in
deciding the exact topics to work on, based on their own interest,
expertise and the suitability of the survey material for the
particular topic.  They are also expected to take the lead in
follow-up proposals and observations.


Applicants should have a PhD in a relevant field of astrophysics, 
preferably with experience in the analysis and interpretation of 
optical imaging data.

The appointment will be on the RA1A scale, and will
be for up to three years.

Informal enquiries about the position may be directed to 
Dr. Malcolm Bremer, Department of Physics, University of Bristol
(Tel: 44 (0)117 928 8764; e-mail: m.bremer@bristol.ac.uk). 


Applications in writing together with a CV and description of research
interests should be sent to the 
Personnel Office (EO), Senate House, Bristol BS8 1TH, quoting
reference 6272, to be received by  31-May-2000.
Applicants should arrange for two referees to send letters of
