support the TGPAP charter of "blurring the boundaries" between
astrophysics and plasma physics.  The response so far to this
conference, however, has not been overwhelming.  We need to be
<underline>ACTIVE</underline> in bringing plasma physics and

Conference on Laboratory Astrophysics with Intense Lasers."  The
abstract submission deadline has been extended by 2 weeks to allow
additional participation.  If you can't attend for the full 3 days,

Dear Colleague,


This is the final announcement for the <italic>3rd International
Conference on Laboratory Astrophysics with Intense Lasers</italic>, to
be held March 30-April 1, 2000 on the campus of Rice University in
Houston, Texas.  This conference is sponsored by the APS Topical Group
for Plasma Astrophysics, and is a continuation of the very successful
1st and 2nd workshops, held in 1996 in Pleasanton, California and in
1998 in Tucson, Arizona.  The goal of this conference is to foster
common areas of research between the fields of high energy-density
plasma physics (accessed by intense lasers and pulsed power facilities)
and astrophysics.  Please join us in actively removing artificial
scientific boundaries separating these two communities by attending
this unique conference.


The availability of intense laser facilities is increasing
dramatically.  Construction of the 2 MJ National Ignition Facility
(NIF) laser in the US, the 60 kJ Ligne d'Integration Laser (LIL) in
France, the 100 TW-10 Hz Kansai Laser in Japan, and petawatt lasers in
the UK and in France is well underway.  Plans for the 2 MJ LMJ laser in
France, and petawatt lasers in Japan and in Germany are also
progressing.  Given this large laser development effort worldwide, the
time is right to pursue developing an astrophysics component to the
research being done on existing lasers and being planned for these new
facilities.  


The potential for genuinely beneficial astrophysics research on intense
lasers looks very promising indeed.  Areas of research accessible
include compressible hydrodynamic mixing, strong shock phenomena,
radiative shocks and jets, magnetohydrodynamics, relativistic plasma
effects, complex opacities, equations of state, dense plasma atomic
physics, x-ray photoionized plasmas, and the generation of ultrastrong
magnetic fields.  


To further focus attention on this emerging research area, we are
convening the <italic>3rd International Conference on Laboratory
Astrophysics with Intense Lasers</italic>, to be held March 30-April 1,
2000 at Rice University.  The abstract deadline has been extended to
Feb. 15, 2000 to coincide with the registration deadline.  Please
submit abstracts and register through the conference website
(http://spacsun.rice.edu/lasers2000) or contact the Conference
Administrator, Umbe Cantu
(<underline><color><param>0000,0000,00FF</param><bigger>umbe@rice.edu</bigger></color></underline>).
 Further information will be posted on our website:
