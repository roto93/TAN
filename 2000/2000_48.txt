Applications are invited for a postdoctoral fellowship in theoretical
astrophysics beginning Summer 2000 for an initial period of two years.
Depending on the availability of funding and satisfactory performance, a
third year may be possible. This position is funded by the NASA Astrophysics
Theory Program for a multidimensional study of accretion and thermonuclear
runaways on white dwarfs and neutron stars. The research will be done in
collaboration with Dr.'s A. Mezzacappa, W. R. Hix, and M. Smith at the Oak
Ridge National Laboratories. Preference will be given to individuals with
evidence of theoretical expertise in multidimensional calculations with
respect to stellar evolution. Astronomers at ASU also have access to the
observational facilities of the Steward Observatories. A Ph.D. in a related
field is required. Funds will be available for travel and other research
expenses. Interested applicants must submit a curriculum vitae, list of
publications, a statement of research interests and have three letters of
reference sent to Sumner Starrfield, at the above address, by 29 February
2000 and the 15th and 30th of each month, thereafter until filled. Email
submission is encouraged. Women and minorities are encouraged to apply.
