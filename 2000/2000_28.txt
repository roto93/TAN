Dear Colleague:

With the event of ten years since the launch of the Hubble Space
Telescope (occurring on April 24, 2000), the Space Telescope Science
Institute will devote its annual (normally in May) spring Symposium to:
"A Decade of HST Science."  This Symposium will review many of the main
scientific results obtained with HST, their impact on astronomy and
astrophysics, and their meaning for future research.

The format for this symposium will consist of 30 minute talks, including 
ample time for discussion following each talk.  There will also be
poster papers.  The topics for the talks and all of the speakers have
been chosen.  A provisional agenda of the program is enclosed.  We do
feel strongly, that the Symposium will greatly benefit if you can
participate.  If you have new results which you would like to present we 
strongly encourage you to present a poster.

If you can and would like to attend the symposium you may register
electronically (at http://www.stsci.edu/isd/Decade/decade.htm) or fill
out the enclosed registration form and mail it (no later than March 1,
1999) to Lorraine Garcia, Symposium Coordinator, Space Telescope Science
Institute, 3700 San Martin Drive, Baltimore, MD  21218, or contact
Lorraine at (410) 338 4402 or send e-mail to garcia@stsci.edu.  To help
defray some of the workshop expenses there is a registration fee of $200
($225 after March 1, 1999,) which includes the symposium dinner but does
not include the proceedings.  If you plan to present a poster paper
please attach the title.

We hope very much to see you here in Baltimore in April.

Sincerely,

Mario Livio
For the Organizing Committee
