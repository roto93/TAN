> IAMS Lecture Announcement 
> 
> Title: Interstellar Molecules and High-resolution Spectroscopy: From   
> Hydrogen to Amino Acid 
> Speaker: Prof. T. Amano (Dept. of Chemistry and Dept. of Physics and   
> Astronomy, The University of Waterloo, Canada) 
> Time: 10:30AM, Oct. 9 (Friday) 
> Place: C.T. Chang Memorial Hall (4F), IAMS  (中研院原分所4樓張昭鼎紀念 
> 講堂　台大校園內) 
> Contact: 陳應誠博士 
> Tel: 23624943 
> 
> 
> Abstract: 
> 
> Fairly complex molecules are known to exist in various interstellar   
> molecular clouds and circumstellar envelopes. In order to identify   
> these species, laboratory spectroscopy data are indispensable. The   
> astronomical observations and identifications of molecular species   
> are ultimate remote sensing. In this talk, before discussing the   
> laboratory spectroscopy, a brief overview of interstellar molecular   
> clouds will be presented. The interplay between the laboratory   
> spectroscopy, mostly microwave to submillimeter-wave regions, and   
> astronomical observations will be discussed by showing some   
> historical developments. Finally I will discuss some recent   
> laboratory submillimeter-wave results on molecular cations as well   
> as molecular anions.
