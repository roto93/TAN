Asia-Pacific Youth Astronomy Camp 2009
 
」」」」」  Third Announcement  」」」」」
 
The on-line application of the Asia-Pacific Youth Astronomy Camp 2009 will 
be closed on April 30th. We encourage students who have interest in the camp 
to submit their applications as soon as possible!  

This camp is hosted by the Institute of Astronomy, National Central 
University in Taiwan. The main theme of the camp is "Evolution of Universe," 
and it includes broad topics of evolution ranging from large-scale 
structure, galaxies, stars, planets, and life. We sincerely welcome graduate 
and undergraduate students from all over the world to join in.

The lecturers and provisional topics which were confirmed by the SOC are 
listed below:
 
# Stellar evolution and solar system
Dina Prialnik, Tel Aviv University, Israel
 
# Proto-stellar disk
Yuri Aikawa, Kobe University, Japan

# ISM and stellar formation
Young Chol Minh, Korea Astronomy and Space Institute, Korea

# Progress on instrument development
Zhiqiang Shen, Shanghai Astronomical Observatory, China

# Milky Way; Black hole
Xiaojun Jiang, National Astronomical Observatory, China

# Extraterrestrial life
Chung-Ming Ko, National Central University, Taiwan

# VLBI; AGN
Makoto Inoue, National Astronomical Observatory of Japan, Japan

# Cosmology 
Jiun-Huei Proty Wu, National Taiwan University, Taiwan 

For more information, please see the official website: 

http://apyac2009.astro.ncu.edu.tw/
