Asia-Pacific Youth Astronomy Camp 2009
 
」」」」」 Second Announcement 」」」」」
 
The official website of the Asia-Pacific Youth Astronomy Camp has already 
opened, and the on-line application also begins. For more information about 
the camp, please visit our website. Any questions and comments are also 
welcome.
 
This camp is hosted by the Institute of Astronomy, National Central 
University in Taiwan. The main theme of the camp is "Evolution of Universe," 
and it includes broad topics of evolution ranging from large-scale 
structure, galaxies, stars, planets, and life. We sincerely welcome graduate 
and undergraduate students from all over the world to join in.


Official Website: http://apyac2009.astro.ncu.edu.tw

Camp Date: July 6 - 10, 2009
 
Number of Participants: 120 people

Requirement of Participants: undergraduate and graduate students

* Although participants' majors are not limit to be astronomy or physics, 
basic astronomical knowledge is essential for participanting this camp.

* First-year graduate students (PhD or Master) are especially welcome.

Fees: Participants just need to pay for their own traffic fares. Foreign 
students have to pay for their own visas to enter Taiwan.

Application Deadline: April 30, 2009
