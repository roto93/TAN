The 2017 Scientific Assembly of the Astronomical Society of the 
Republic of China (ASROC2017) will be held at National United 
University on May 19 (Friday) ¡V 21 (Sunday), 2017.  This is an annual 
event for all astronomers and astrophysicists in Taiwan to present 
their most recent discoveries and observations, to report their latest 
research results, to exchange ideas, and to organize collaborations. 
Oral presentations and conference posters in all areas of astronomy 
and astrophysics are solicited.  ASROC2017 is also an excellent venue 
for students, in particular graduate students, to present their 
research findings or progress from their thesis studies. 

The meeting will include plenary talks, scientific and educational 
sessions, poster presentation, and a business meeting.  This year, the 
featured plenary speakers are 1) Prof. Christopher Russell of 
University of California, Los Angeles.  Prof. Russell is the principal 
investigator of the Dawn mission to the asteroids Vesta and Ceres.  He 
is the acting System-wide Director of Institute of Geophysics and 
Planetary Physics (IGPP), the head of the Space Physics Center in 
IGPP, UCLA and the Director of the UCLA Branch of the California Space 
Grant Consortium.  He has also led and participated in many space 
missions, such as POLAR, Rosetta, STEREO and THEMIS.  His plenary talk 
will be ¡§The Dawn Mission to Vesta and Ceres: A Mission in Space and 
Time.¡¨  2) Prof. Gabriela Gonzalez, Department of Physics and 
Astronomy, Louisiana State University.  Prof. Gonzalez is the 
spokesperson of LIGO Scientific Collaboration.  Her works focus on the 
detection of gravitational waves.  She will present ¡§Searching for - 
and Finding! - Gravitational Waves.¡¨ 

ASROC2017 website is now open for registration.  Participants of the 
scientific assembly should submit their abstracts and register 
on-line.  A registration fee of NT$1,000 (NT$800 for students) should 
be paid on site. 

The Astronomical Society cordially invites you to participate in this 
annual event with all the many interesting and exciting activities. 
For more details and updated information, please visit the ASROC 
website at http://www.asroc.org.tw/.

Call for abstract for APRIM 2017 (Deadline March 1, 2017) 

Dear colleagues: 

The 13th Asian-Pacific Regional IAU Meeting (APRIM) will be 
held at the International Convention Center in Taipei, Taiwan on 
July 3-7, 2017. 

APRIM is authorized by the International Astronomical Union (IAU) 
to be held every three years with the aim to promote scientific 
activity and collaboration in the Asian-Pacific region.  This is the 
first first time Taiwan hosts an APRIM. 

We would like to encourage all astronomers in Taiwan to attend 
this APRIM meeting and to present your latest results to our 
colleagues and friends in neighboring regions. 

The APRIM 2017 website is now open for registration and abstract 
submission. The deadline for abstract submission is March 1, 2017. 
Please check details here: http://www.aprim2017.tw/ 
We hope to see you at APRIM 2017. 

Best regards, 
You-Hua Chu 
on behalf of the SOC and LOC of APRIM2017
