> (1) Background and Scope:
> This is an international symposium celebrating the 30th anniversary of =
Nobeyama Radio Observatory (NRO) of National Astronomical Observatory of =
Japan (NAOJ), which was established in 1982.=20
> The scope of the symposium is as follows:
> =20
> - Recent progress of radio astronomical observations, including    =
first outcomes from the ALMA early science, in cosmology, galaxies, star =
and planet formation, evolved stars, the solar system, interstellar =
matter, and other fields.
> - Related observations in the other wavelengths and theoretical =
studies.
> - On-going and future radio astronomy projects which are complimentary =
to ALMA, as well as the next international projects after ALMA.
> =20
> (2) Venue:
> The symposium will be held in The Prince Hakone, which is located by =
Ashi Lake in Hakone, Japan.
> http://www.princehotels.com/en/the_prince_hakone/
> Hakone, part of the Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park, is located about =
100 km south-westward from Tokyo.  It is a popular sightseeing area =
where visitors can enjoy natural beauty and scenic views including Mt. =
Fuji, Ashi Lake and Owaku Valley.  It takes approximately 4 hours by =
train and bus from the Narita Airport. We will show more details on the =
access to the venue later on.
