                Astronomy Collquium
             
               Institute of Astronomy
           National Tsing Hua University

The Single Degenerate Progenitor Channel of Type Ia Supernovae
 
                  Prof. Ronald Taam
              (Northwestern University)
         
          12:10pm, July 9, 2004 (Friday)
             Room 620, Phys Bldg, NTHU

Abstract

Progenitor models for Type Ia supernovae are described.
Particular emphasis is focused on the single degenerate channel in which a
white dwarf accretes mass from an slightly evolved non degenerate
star. The numerical results reveal that CO white dwarfs more massive than
about 0.8 solar masses can accrete sufficient mass to evolve to a Type Ia
supernova phase. For a narrow range of parameter space sufficient helium
may be accumulated such that sub Chandrasekhar mass models for Type Ia
supernovae are possible.
