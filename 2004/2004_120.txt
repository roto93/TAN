Research Fellows in Astronomy and Astrophysics
Institute of Astronomy and Astrophysics
Academia Sinica, Taiwan

The Academia Sinica Institute of Astronomy and Astrophysics (ASIAA,
http://www.asiaa.sinica.edu.tw/) has openings for one or more research
fellows.

Depending on experience, these positions can be occupied at the levels of
assistant, associate, or full fellows.  Candidates with Ph.D. degrees and
postdoctoral experience in theoretical, observational, or instrumental
astronomy are invited to apply for these positions.

ASIAA is an institute of Academia Sinica, the national research organization
in Taiwan.  The institute has a staff of approximately 100, and has operations
in both Taipei and Hawaii.  Present research at the ASIAA includes the Solar
System, Stellar Evolution, Star Formation, Interstellar Chemistry, Galactic
Dynamics, Active Galaxies, and Cosmology. We are partners in the SubMillimeter
Array (SMA) project on Mauna Kea, and are planning to join the Atacama Large
Millimeter Array (ALMA) project through Japan.  ASIAA also participates in the
CFHT Wide Field Infrared Camera development in exchange for observing time on
the telescope.  A 4-telescope system is under construction in Lulin Mountain
in Taiwan to conduct an occultation survey in search for small Kuiper Belt
objects.  An Array for Microwave Background Anisotropy (AMiBA) in being built
on Mauna Loa.  We also have access to the Arizona Radio Observatory 10-m and
12-m telescopes at Kitt Peak and Mt. Graham. An increasing level of theoretical
and computational astrophysics is being pursued through the establishment of
Theoretical Institute for Advanced Research in Astrophysics (TIARA) in
collaboration with National Tsing Hua University.

We seek scientists to augment both the Institute's science and engineering
efforts, in particular: (1) infrared and millimeter-wave astronomers
interested in molecule and grain chemistry in the interstellar medium;
(2) astronomers with experience in radio interferometry to participate in
ongoing tests/software development and scientific observations with the SMA
and ALMA; (3) optical and infrared astronomers to initiate observing programs
with ground and space-based telescopes; (4) solar system and planetary
astronomers to participate in the search for Kuiper Belt Objects through
the Taiwan-America Occultation Survey; (5) scientists and engineers to
participate in instrumentation development for SMA and ALMA; and (6)
scientists with backgrounds in astronomy, astrophysics, physics, and planetary
sciences to pursue topics in theoretical astrophysics, computational fluid
dynamics, and magnetohydrodynamics.

Applicants should submit a curriculum vitae, a brief summary of research and
future research plans or instrumentation experience, and arrange three
letters of recommendation to: Dr. Sun Kwok, director, ASIAA, PO Box 23-141,
Taipei 106, Taiwan; Fax: 886-2-2367 7849; Email: asiaa@asiaa.sinica.edu.tw.
The closing date for applications is January 31, 2005.

Institute of Astronomy and Astrophysics

ASIAA SENIOR MICROWAVE TECHNICIAN
Academia Sinica Institute of Astronomy and Astrophysics (ASIAA)

I. SUMMARY OF DUTIES: Regular, Full-Time, Non-Civil Service position with the
   Academia Sinica Institute of Astronomy and Astrophysics (ASSIA), Hawaii
   Operations, located in Hilo, Hawaii. Continuation of employment is
   dependent upon program/operational needs, satisfactory work performance,
   and availability of funds.

   This position is in support of the works and the upkeep of existing
   instruments for the SubMillimeter Array (SMA) and the Array for Microwave
   Background Anisotrophy (AMiBA) projects.  Incumbent will provide design,
   testing, installation and maintenance support to the IF/LO system during
   the development, commissioning and operation phases of the various projects
   of ASIAA.  The incumbent will take the lead role in the microwave-related
   programs at the site.  Other duties include inventory maintenance of
   supplies, documenting modifications to systems, and construction of new
   receiver components and systems under the direction of the engineering
   staff.  May also work along with scientific staff as necessary.  Work may
   be required outside the normal 40-hour workweek.


II. LEVEL OF INTERACTION: Works in the ASIAA Hawaii Operations group as a
    member of the team responsible for the instrumentation systems on both the
    SMA and ASIAA projects. Interacts closely with other members of the ASIAA
    team, members of the Smithsonian Astronomical Observatory (SAO) team
    (partners in the SMA), and with visiting scientists from other groups.
    The incumbent will need to work unsupervised, once job tasks have been
    described.  Often, the incumbent will be the responsible project
    representative at the telescope site and need to take a leadership role in
    carrying through the daily project work tasks.  The incumbent may serve as
    the responsible safety representative for ASIAA at the telescope sites and
    must interact with other agency staff.  Will need to produce some
    documentation, examples of maintenance procedures, modifications made to
    instruments, for reference by other staff. Requires strong problem solving
    skills.  Work is carried out in the office at Hilo (occasionally), at the
    summit of Mauna Kea at 14,000 ft. elevation, and at the Mauna Loa
    Observatory site at 12,000 ft. elevation.  Roughly 80% of the workdays
    will be at one of the mountain sites.

III. MAJOR DUTIES & RESPONSIBILITIES (* Essential Job Functions-Major Duties &
    Responsibilities are listed in order of importance):

70%* Microwave instrumentation.  Incumbent will provide design, testing,
     installation and maintenance support to the IF/LO system during the
     development, commissioning and operation phases of the SMA and AMiBA.
     This includes a wide range of microwave components and subsystems
     developed at the ASIAA facility in Taipei, Taiwan, and at the
     collaborator's facilities, in support of observatory and telescopes
     operation on Mauna Kea; diagnosing errors and faults in the IF/LO system;
     replacing and or repairing microwave components, electronic components,
     cables as needed; installing discrete components, printed circuit boards,
     and cables into higher level assemblies; assembling and installing
     equipment racks, chassis, subassemblies, power cabling, coaxial cables
     etc; installing and testing the subassemblies; developing, constructing
     and testing prototype low-noise amplifier; power supply and control
     circuits for use in phase-stable reference sources and phase-lock loops
     at RF and microwave frequencies; fabricating complex thru-hole and
     surface-mount printed circuit board assemblies from schematics and verbal
     instructions; constructing multi-conductor and coaxial cables and cable
     assemblies; installing discrete components, printed circuit boards and
     interconnecting cables into higher-level assemblies using a wide variety
     of fasteners, fixtures and clamping devices; developing, fabricating,
     assembling and wiring control panels and junction boxes;

10%* Documentation.  Writes and draws documentation of computer and CAD
     systems. Prepares CAD drawings for microwave projects as required.

10%* Component research & procurement.  Assists Engineering staff in component
     and material research for telescope spares or projects and follows
     through with procurement when necessary.

10%  Performs other duties as assigned.


IV. MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS:

A. Education: Associate's degree as a microwave technician or similar, or a
   formal training program (i.e. apprenticeship program) with demonstrated
   ability to perform the essential job functions.

B. Experience: At least five years postgraduate experience as a microwave
   technician.  Experience with operations on a remote site.  Experience in
   working with minimal supervision.  Previous experience or familiarity with
   workplace safety.

C. Abilities, Knowledge, and Skills: Demonstrated theoretical and practical
   knowledge of the concepts, principles, techniques and practices of
   electronic theory, with particular emphasis on complex microwave
   instrumentation; ability to work from schematics, sketches, prints and
   verbal instructions to fabricate prototype and/or final electronic systems;
   knowledge of modem, computer-based instrumentation such as VME, VXI and
   GPIB; knowledge of at least one high-level computer programming language,
   preferably C and its application to control and data acquisition systems,
   ability to read and utilize schematic drawings, mechanical assembly
   drawings and parts lists and the ability to operate basic hand and power
   tools, including soldering irons, drill presses, etc., safely and
   efficiently.  Excellent communication skills - verbal and written.  Must
   hold a valid current driving license and be able to drive 4-wheel drive
   vehicles with manual transmission.  Proficient problem solving skills
   appropriate to the job; able to work as part of a team.

D. Physical and/or Medical Demands: Most duties must be performed at 14,000 ft
   elevation (Mauna Kea) or at 12,000 ft. elevation (Mauna Loa).  The incumbent
   must be able to carry out his/her duties effectively at this elevation.  A
   high-altitude physical may be required after hiring.

V. DESIRABLE QUALIFICATIONS: Knowledge of single-mode optical fiber technology
   including installation techniques, fusion and mechanical splicing and
   high-resolution time-domain reflectometry, is highly desirable; knowledge
   of optical components and techniques including lasers, lenses and fiber
   optic devices would be advantageous.  Experience of operations in a
   telescope or similar environment, direct experience of maintenance work is
   desirable.

VI. Inquiries:
   Dr. Ming-Tang Chen, (808) 961-3652, mchen@asiaa.sinica.edu.tw.  This
   position is open until filled.  Salary commensurate with abilities and
   experience.
