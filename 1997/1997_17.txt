Dear TANese,

In the next few days there will be several astronomers from overseas to
visit the ASIAA (Institute of Astronomy & Astrophysics, Academia Sinica)
for about a week or so, including some MAAC (Members of the ASIAA
Advisory Committee), big-name chinese astronomers.   

Here is a quick preview of upcoming ASIAA visitors and seminars of this
month:

Dr Tom Wilson, the first ASIAA Visiting Scholar of 1997, just finished
his successful and enjoyable one-week visit in Taiwan, and left on 3
March.  He had an intensive programme of giving talks at ASIAA, NTNU, and
NCU as well as discussion sessions with local astronomers.  


[Feb - late April]
Prof Sun Kwok, from the Univ of Calgary (Canada), MAAC, expert of
infrared astronomy and planetary nebulae, has been with us since early
February, and will carry on his research here until late April.


[20-26/Mar]
Prof Yuk-Ling Yung of the Division of Geological & Planetary Sci.,
California Institute of Technology, MAAC, will visit Taiwan from 20-26/Mar.
At 15:30 20/Mar Thursday, he will give a talk on

	"Titan: An atmosphere in a state of flux"


[20-28/Mar]
Prof Fred KY Lo, MAAC, ASIAA director, will be around during 20-28/Mar.  

 
[22-26/Mar]
Another MAAC, Dr Paul Ho from Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory,
Harvard, will visit us from 22-26/Mar.  He will give an informal talk on
his search for brown dwarfs at 10:30AM 24/Mar Monday.


[26/Mar 10:30am]
On 26/Mar Wednesday at 10:30AM, Dr Hyung Mok Lee at Pusan National
University (Korea), will give us some input on

	"Distribution and Dynamics of Interstellar Clouds toward the 
	Galactic Center"

He is invited by the Inst. of astronomy, NCU.


[27/Mar-06/Apr]
Dr Ohashi at Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory can be seen at ASIAA
from Mar 27 to Apr 06 on his way to Japan for an observing trip, he is
expected to give a talk during his stay.  


[28/Mar 10:30am]
Dr Hsiang-Kuang Chang, working with Prof Dean-Yi Chou at NTHU, will
present the progress of their current project, which is about

	"Acoustic Imaging in Helioseismology"

at 10:30AM 28/Mar Friday.
