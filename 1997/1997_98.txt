{\bf Scope of the symposium}\\
The physics of Wolf-Rayet stars has major highlights: they are the most
advanced observed stage of massive star evolution, progenitors of
supernovae and sources of cosmic rays.  They are the result of massive star
evaporation, and provide nucleosynthesis tests.  Just as WR stars are a
particular phase in the evolution of massive stars, WR galaxies are a stage
in the evolution of the dominant starburst stellar population in dwarf
irregular galaxies or \Hii galaxies.  During the last decade the major role
of starbursts in the evolution of the universe has become evident.  Recent
years have seen avalanches of new observational results in the field of WR
stars and WR galaxies from space observatories like {\sl IUE, HST, HUT,
ROSAT, ASCA, GRO} and {\sl ISO}, from the airborne observatory {\sl KAO},
and from ground-based (adaptive optics) optical, IR, and radio
observatories.  {\it E.g.}, new observations in the centre of our own
Galaxy reveal an large number of hot luminous helium-rich stars of WR-type
and Of/WN-type, whose winds may have a profound influence on the evolution
of the Galactic Centre.  The spectroscopic WR phenomenon allows massive
starbursts to be observed out to huge distances, and thus to be studied at
great variety.  While these observational developments are taking place on
both the extragalactic and stellar fronts, also on the theoretical and
modeling side there are important developments in atmosphere modeling
techniques, galaxy/starburst spectral synthesizing and stellar evolution
modeling.  Aspects of the astrophysical problems to be discussed at
the symposium are:
\vspace*{-3mm}
\begin{quote}
1. Basic observational properties of WR stars and other hot massive stars. \\
2. Models for single star evolution of massive stars:
   winds + atmospheres + interior.  \\
3. Interaction of WR stars and other hot massive stars with their environment:
   colliding winds and ring nebulae.  \\
4. WR stars and other hot massive stars in the Galactic Center and in Local
   Group giant \Hii regions (individual stars).  \\
5. WR stars and other massive stars in starburst galaxies.  The case of WR
   galaxies (integrated spectra).\\
6. Starbursts in normal and active galaxies: their effects on the ISM and on chemical evolution.\\
\end{quote}
\vspace*{-8mm}
A preliminary scientific programme is given in Annex A.

We will benefit from the importance of IAU Symposia in bringing together
specialists in different fields and associated with different Divisions and
Commissions.  The symposium will join the flourishing fields of hot
luminous stars with dense supersonic stellar winds, hydrodynamics of
wind-wind and wind-shell interaction, interstellar matter, high energy
astrophysics, birth and evolution of massive stars and galactic evolution.

{\bf Venue} \\
After three IAU Symposia related to hot massive stars on the American
continents (No.  49 in 1971 in Argentina, No.  83 in 1978 in Canada, No.
99 in 1981 in Mexico), two in Europe (No.  116 in 1985 in Greece and No.
163 in 1994 in Italy), and one in Asia (No.  143 in 1990 in Indonesia), we
are delighted that IAU Symposium No.  193 brings us to Mexico again, this
time on the Pacific coast.  We chose Puerto Vallarta, because it is unique
in offering the beauty of a beach resort in a tropical forest setting
together with the charm of a colonial town.  For local information, we
refer to guidebooks such as Lonely Planet and Insight Guide, and to the
links provided in our Web site.

The Hotel Camino Real in Puerto Vallarta has been selected for its quiet
and beautiful location, its friendly service and its good food.  All
symposium participants will stay in that hotel on the basis of full board.
A block booking for rooms has been made for all participants, who are
advised to arrive in Puerto Vallarta not later than Monday November 2, and
to depart from Puerto Vallarta not earlier than Sunday November 8.  Note
that the working sessions will be from Tuesday through Saturday.

The package-deal price from Monday 2 November, evening, to Sunday 9
November, morning, is {\em US\$}~780.-- per participant, in case of single
room occupancy.  If sharing a double room, the cost per participant is
reduced to {\em US\$}~550.--.  Children between 4 and 12 will pay only {\em
US\$}~160.--.  These prices include lodging, all meals with beverages, the
welcome party and the special conference dinner.  They do not include the
Thursday afternoon excursion and the following supper, to allow
participants to stroll around town and explore other restaurants on that
evening.  An accommodation pre-payment of {\em US\$}~100.-- per room is
required, to be payed together with the registration fee, before April 15,

Early November offers the best climate in Puerto Vallarta, being after the
