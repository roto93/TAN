Applications are invited for a Post Doctoral Scholar/Senior Post
Doctoral Scholar position with the Infrared Astronomy group at the the
California Institute of Technology.  It is possible that a more senior
appointment, at the level of a Senior Research Fellow, would be made
based on the background and experience of the successful applicant.
Broad areas of research are possible utilizing the capabilities of the
telescopes available to the group, which include the 5m Hale telescope
and the telescopes of the W.M. Keck Observatory.  In addition
investigations with HST and ISO are among the programs being undertaken
by members of this group.  The major observational programs of the
Caltech infrared group consist of studies of Active Galaxies including
infrared luminous galaxies, radio galaxies, and quasars, deep imaging
at infrared wavelengths, and speckle imaging of nearby AGNs.

Candidates should have a Ph.D. (or equivalent) in astronomy, phyics or
a related field.  Appointment is contingent upon evidence of completion
of a Ph.D.  Annual salary can vary according the applicant's
qualifications and experience.  Postdoctoral/Senior Postdoctoral
scholar positions are awarded initially for a one year period.
Appointments may be renewed for a maximum of two (2) additional years.
An appointment at the Senior Research Fellow level will require 2-4
years experience beyond the Ph.D.

Please send a brief statement of research interests, a curricumlum vita,
biblography and names of three scientists who can be contacted  for
letters of reference to the above address by 15 February 98.  Email
submission is encouraged. The appointment is expected to begin in the
spring/summer of 1998.  AAE/EOE
