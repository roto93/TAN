                       ------  Colloquium  -------

TIME:    10:30am, 14/Jun/1997 Saturday

TITLE:   "New Developments in Submillimeter Astronomy Instrumentation"  

PLACE:   Conference Room, ASIAA (Biochemistry Building, 4th floor)

SPEAKER:  Prof Jonas Zmuidzinas
	  Department of Physics, Caltech  

Prof Zmuidzinas will also give an after-lunch talk on 

	"Airborne Submillimeter Observations of Interstellar Water" 

at 13:30, 13/Jun/1997 Friday.


	"Professor Jonas Zmuidzinas of the Physics department of 
	Caltech is a world leader in building submillimeter 
	receivers for observations on air-borne platforms such as 
	the Kuiper Airborne Observatory (now defunct) and the
	Stratospheric Observatory for Far Infrared Astronomy (SOFIA 
	- 3 m telescope carried by a modified Boeing 747.)  His 
	scientific interests have been to study submillimeter
	ionic/atomic (e.g. CII/CI) and molecular (eg. H_2O) 
	transitions from the interstellar medium.
 
	He and his students have built the best 800 GHz quasi-optic 
	SIS receiver with a planar antenna (instead of the more
	conventional wave-guide and horn arrangement.)  His 
	receiver may well be the best at 800 GHz independent of 
	design.
 
	He is an excellent physicist with very good physical 
	intuition."
                   
                                   --- K.Y. Lo, Director of ASIAA
